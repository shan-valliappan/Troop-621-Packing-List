⚠️ CRITICAL TRAVEL & CAMP RULES

🎒

One-Trip Rule

Carry all gear yourself in one trip. Expect a 1-2 mile hike-in. Everything must fit inside or attach to your 65-70L pack.

👔

Uniform on Departure

Wear full Class "A" Scout Uniform (shirt, patches, pants, slide, neckerchief, boots). Wear Class "B" underneath to switch easily.

📵

No Cell Phones

Virtually zero cell coverage inside the campground. Leave all cellular devices safely at home.

🥾

Strict Footwear Rule

No open-toed shoes or slip-ons at camp. Crocs are strictly restricted to the waterfront or shower house only.

Crucial
❌

No Cotton ("Cotton is Rotten!")

Cotton absorbs moisture and dries extremely slowly. Use synthetics, polyester, or merino wool for socks/base layers.

🏷️

Label Everything

Put your name on ALL gear, jackets, and clothing items to prevent loss or mix-ups in camp.

📋 1. Administrative & Paperwork

Pre-Camp
Current BSA Medical Forms (Parts A & B)

Carry two (2) physical printed copies in your pack. (Required for both Scouts and Adults).

BSA Parental Firearms Permission Form

Carry four (4) physical printed copies inside your backpack.

Swim Test Certification

Ensure your swim test is fully completed, logged, and registered before leaving.

Medications

Original prescription bottles inside a clear zip-lock bag labeled with clear instructions.

🎒 2. The Ten Essentials

Always in Daypack
First Aid Kit

Bandaids, moleskin, and antiseptic wipes. (Check expiry dates!)

Warm Extra Clothing

Lightweight fleece or sweater (NO COTTON) and packable down jacket.

Pocket Knife / Multi-tool

Must carry Tot'n Chip card and follow all safety rules.

Compass

Fluid-filled baseplate compass for orienteering.

Sun Protection

Sunscreen (SPF 30+), sunglasses, lip balm with SPF, and wide-brimmed sun hat.

Matches / Fire Starter

Waterproof match case or magnesium firesteel. (Must carry Firem'n Chit card).

Rain Gear

Sturdy, breathable water-resistant jacket & pants (NO cheap ponchos!).

Water Hydration

Two (2) wide-mouth Nalgene 1L+ bottles (2L min). No single-use plastic/heavy metal.

Emergency Food

High-energy snacks (trail mix, beef jerky, energy bars) to share with "mini-bears."

Emergency Shelter

Lightweight aluminized space blanket, emergency bivy, or ground tarp.

⛺ 3. Main Gear & Sleep System

Primary Gear
Main Backpack

65–70 Liters capacity (REI professional torso fitting is highly recommended).

Pack Rain Cover

Sized properly to completely cover your pack with your sleep pad strapped outside.

Sleeping Bag

Rated to 20°F or lower, lightweight and compressible (synthetic preferred).

Pillow (Highly Recommended)

Compact inflatable camping pillows work great.

Sleeping Pad

Z-folding foam pad (e.g., Therm-a-Rest Z-Lite) or self-inflating pad (bring patch kit).

Headlamp or Flashlight

High quality with fresh batteries installed, plus one set of spares.

Mess Kit (NO paper/metal)

Lightweight plastic BPA-free plate, bowl, insulated cup with lid, and cutlery set.

Camp Chair

Ultra-lightweight, compact, and foldable (no bulky classic lawn/bag chairs).

Carabiners (2–4 sturdy)

3–4 inch utility clips to attach gear (like water bottles) securely to pack.

Whistle / Signal Mirror

Safety whistle attached to your pack shoulder strap with a lanyard.

👕 4. Clothing to Pack

No Cotton!
3–4 Class "B" Activity Shirts

Mango Troop t-shirts are the preferred daily uniform of the day in camp.

3 Pairs of Socks

High-quality merino wool or synthetic active blends (STRICTLY NO COTTON!).

3 Pairs of Underwear

Synthetic/athletic moisture-wicking material (no cotton to avoid chafing).

1 Pair of Pants

Heavy-duty long pants (Official Scout uniform pants or quick-dry trail pants).

1–2 Pairs of Shorts

Active Scout uniform shorts or rugged synthetic utility hiking shorts.

Swimsuit

Boardshorts or quick-drying swim shorts (crucial for waterfront/swim test).

Jacket

High-loft warm fleece jacket and/or a compressible puffy jacket.

Camp / Water Shoes

Closed-toe water shoes or Crocs (ONLY used in showers or down at waterfront).

Cold Weather Gear

Beanie (knit cap) and lightweight gloves for chilly mornings and nights.

Pajamas / Base Layer

Clean polyester/merino wool set reserved solely for sleeping (e.g., Costco 32° Heat).

🧼 5. Personal Toiletries & Camp Extras

Personal Care
Towel

Quick-dry microfiber travel towel (avoid thick, bulky bath towels).

Soap

Bar soap in a secure plastic travel case or biodegradable liquid body wash.

Dental Care

Toothbrush and travel-sized toothpaste tube.

Deodorant

Stick style (unscented preferred so you don't attract bugs or bears!).

Insect Repellent

Non-aerosol spray/lotion only (aerosols damage synthetic tent fly fabrics).

Scout Handbook & Booklet Docs

Kept safe inside a gallon zip-lock bag, alongside your active Merit Badge books.

Writing Supplies

Small pocket notebook and pen/pencil (required for several camp badges).

Trading Post Money

$50–$70 cash in a labeled envelope/wallet for souvenirs, craft kits, and snacks.

Camera (Optional)

Small standalone film/digital camera (remember, phones are banned!).

Travel Games (Optional)

Deck of playing cards or quick travel board games to enjoy during rest hours.

Personal Camp Lantern (Optional)

Handy to hang inside your tent to read or organize gear at night.
