⚠️ CRITICAL TRAVEL & CAMP RULES

🎒

One-Trip Rule

Carry all gear yourself in one trip. Expect a 1-2 mile hike-in. Everything must fit inside or attach to your 65-70L pack.

👔

Uniform on Departure

Wear full Class "A" Scout Uniform (shirt, patches, pants, slide, neckerchief, boots). Wear Class "B" underneath to switch easily.

📵

No Cell Phones

Virtually zero cell coverage inside the campground. Leave all cellular devices safely at home.

🥾

Strict Footwear Rule

No open-toed shoes or slip-ons at camp. Crocs are strictly restricted to the waterfront or shower house only.

Crucial
❌

No Cotton ("Cotton is Rotten!")

Cotton absorbs moisture and dries extremely slowly. Use synthetics, polyester, or merino wool for socks/base layers.

🏷️

Label Everything

Put your name on ALL gear, jackets, and clothing items to prevent loss or mix-ups in camp.

📋 1. Administrative & Paperwork

Pre-Camp
Current BSA Medical Forms (Parts A & B)

Carry two (2) physical printed copies in your pack. (Required for both Scouts and Adults).

BSA Parental Firearms Permission Form

Carry four (4) physical printed copies inside your backpack.

Swim Test Certification

Ensure your swim test is fully completed, logged, and registered before leaving.

Medications

Original prescription bottles inside a clear zip-lock bag labeled with clear instructions.

🎒 2. The Ten Essentials

Always in Daypack
First Aid Kit

Bandaids, moleskin, and antiseptic wipes. (Check expiry dates!)

Warm Extra Clothing

Lightweight fleece or sweater (NO COTTON) and packable down jacket.

Pocket Knife / Multi-tool

Must carry Tot'n Chip card and follow all safety rules.

Compass

Fluid-filled baseplate compass for orienteering.

Sun Protection

Sunscreen (SPF 30+), sunglasses, lip balm with SPF, and wide-brimmed sun hat.

Matches / Fire Starter

Waterproof match case or magnesium firesteel. (Must carry Firem'n Chit card).

Rain Gear

Sturdy, breathable water-resistant jacket & pants (NO cheap ponchos!).

Water Hydration

Two (2) wide-mouth Nalgene 1L+ bottles (2L min). No single-use plastic/heavy metal.

Emergency Food

High-energy snacks (trail mix, beef jerky, energy bars).

Emergency Shelter

Lightweight aluminized space blanket, emergency bivy, or ground tarp.

⛺ 3. Main Gear & Sleep System

Primary Gear
Main Backpack

65–70 Liters capacity (REI professional torso fitting is highly recommended).

Pack Rain Cover

Sized properly to completely cover your pack with your sleep pad strapped outside.

Sleeping Bag

Rated to 20°F or lower, lightweight and compressible (synthetic preferred).

Sleeping Pad

Closed-cell foam or insulated inflatable pad. Critical to block sub-surface ground chill.

Pillow

Lightweight, inflatable camp pillow (or pack clothes inside a clean stuff sack).

👕 4. Clothing & Layers

No Cotton
Synthetic Hiking Shirts

2-3 shirts. Breathable polyester or nylon blend fabrics only.

Convertible Hiking Pants

1-2 pairs of zip-off or standard nylon active trail pants.

Merino Wool / Synthetic Socks

3-4 pairs. Heavy cushions help prevent physical heel blisters.

Waterfront / Shower Shoes

Crocs or water sandals strictly used at the waterfront or shower stalls.

🔦 5. Personal Items & Electronics

Camp Kit
Headlamp / Flashlight

Bring extra sets of lithium/alkaline replacement batteries.

Mess Kit (Plate, Bowl, Spoon)

Lightweight mesh bag with robust reusable clip hook attached.

Scout Handbook & Docs

Keep completely safe inside a gallon-sized heavy zip-lock bag.

Toiletries Kit

Toothbrush, toothpaste, unscented stick deodorant, biodegradeable soap.

Trading Post Money

$50-$70 cash secured inside a labeled pouch for snacks or badges.

Insect Repellent

Non-aerosol lotions/pump sprays only (Aerosols melt synthetic fly covers).
